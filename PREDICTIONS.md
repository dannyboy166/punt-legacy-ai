## How to Analyze Races

When asked to analyze a race, apply common sense using these principles:

### Before You Start

**Slow down. Look for high ratings, but check they're relevant to THIS race before flagging.**

For each rating, ask: "Is this form relevant to the race we're analyzing?"
- Is the distance similar? (within ~20% of today's distance)
- Is it recent enough?
- Is the condition relevant? (adjacent conditions or 2 count)

**Distance relevance gradient:**
- Within 10% = most relevant
- Within 20% = still relevant
- Within 30% = less relevant
- Beyond 30% = limited relevance

High rating + relevant = flag it. High rating + not relevant = ignore it.

---

## Core Principle

**Best ratings at relevant runs.** Think logically - don't overweight any single factor too aggressively.

---

## What to Look For

**First:** Scan for any standout ratings clearly above the field at relevant distance/condition. These should always be considered.

**Then:** Look at who is consistently rating at the top of the field.

Both matter - don't ignore a standout rating, but also value consistency.

### Key Factors

1. **Adj ratings** - The primary metric
2. **Recency** - Recent ratings matter more than old runs
3. **Distance match** - Form at similar distance to today's race
4. **Condition match** - Form at similar/adjacent conditions (see scale below)

---

## Selection Rules

- **Top pick:** Horse whose recent Adj ratings at similar distance/condition are clearly higher than the rest of the field
- **Value angle:** Consider if they have a relevant run or runs clearly above the field - especially with a weight drop
- **First/second up:** Check their historical 1st-up or 2nd-up ratings if applicable

---

## Secondary Factors (Note but don't overweight)

- **Weight changes** - Scale: 1-2kg minor | 3-4kg decent | 5-6kg solid | 7+kg big change
- **Barrier draws** - Inside vs outside at certain tracks/distances
- **Class rise/drop** - Stepping up significantly or down
- **Gear changes** - Blinkers/winkers first time
- **Days since last run** - Backing up quickly vs fresh

## Pace & Position Analysis

When picking winners (not just value), always assess the race dynamics:

### Pace Scenario
- **Hot pace (3+ leaders)** — backmarkers benefit. Leaders tire each other out. Horses settling last with good ratings can storm home.
- **Moderate pace (2 leaders)** — on-pace runners benefit. Sitting 2nd-3rd behind the leader saves energy and gives the ideal run.
- **Soft pace (0-1 leaders)** — the leader gets an easy time up front and is very hard to catch. If your pick leads in soft pace = big advantage.

### Settling Position + Barrier
- A horse that leads (Settles 1) from a wide barrier (10+) has to burn energy crossing to the front — negative.
- A horse that settles midfield from an inside barrier at a tight/short track gets the ideal run — positive.
- At 1000-1200m sprints, inside barriers matter most. At 1600m+, barriers matter less.
- Country tracks are generally tighter-turning than metro — inside barriers more valuable.

### Wet Track Form
- When the track is Soft (5-7) or Heavy (8-10), **prioritise horses with proven wet track form**.
- Some horses are wet track specialists — their ratings jump up on soft/heavy. These are strong picks when it's wet.
- A horse with no soft/heavy race form is an unknown on wet — discount them vs proven wet trackers.

### Course Form vs Interstate Form
- Horses proven at THIS track (course specialists) are more reliable than higher-rated horses from other states/tracks.
- Interstate form doesn't always translate — different tracks, different competition patterns.
- Tested: Billie had 101.3 Adj from VIC but ran 5th at Northam. Guarantor with 101.0 at Northam won — course form was more predictive.

### How to Combine All Factors

For each contender, ask:
1. **Ratings:** What are their recent Adj ratings at similar distance/conditions? (primary)
2. **Pace:** Does the pace scenario suit their running style?
3. **Barrier:** Will they get the run they need from their barrier?
4. **Wet form:** If it's soft/heavy, do they have proven wet form?
5. **Course form:** Have they performed at this track before?
6. **Weight/class:** Are they lighter than recent wins? Dropping in class?

The best picks have multiple factors aligning — not just the highest rating.

### When to Bet vs Skip

**BET when:**
- One horse has clearly better RECENT Adj ratings than the rest of the field at similar distance/conditions
- Multiple factors align (ratings + barrier + wet form + pace)
- There's genuine separation (2+ points of Adj above the next best)

**SKIP when:**
- Top 3-4 horses are within 1 point of each other on recent Adj — it's a coin flip
- No horse has proven form on today's conditions (e.g., all G4 form on an H9 day)
- Too many unknowns (first starters, limited form)
- You're forcing a pick because you feel you should — patience wins

**The best winners we've found had clear edges:**
- Zouslayer: 101.9 with 3 excuses at exact track/condition, next best was 99.5
- Sexy Warrior: 100.2 on H8 with excuses + barrier 2, field had no heavy form
- West Star: 102.7 at exact track + 102.2 on S5, vs fav at 102.4 but no course form

Don't bet every race. Wait for the clear standout.

### Staking ($1-$3 per race max)

**$3 single bet** — High confidence. Clear standout on recent form, multiple factors align, not a huge roughie (under $10). Example: Zouslayer at $7 with 101.9 and 3 excuses at the exact track.

**$2 single bet** — Good pick but minor concerns (wide barrier, untested on surface, one factor not ideal). Or a confident pick at longer odds ($10-$15).

**$1 single bet** — Speculative. The form is there but something holds you back (first time at track, long layoff, competitive field). Or a roughie ($15+) with genuine form.

**Multiple $1 bets** — When 2-3 horses have genuine claims and you can't separate them. Better to spread $2-$3 across the top contenders than load up on one in a competitive race.

**Never bet more than $3 total on a single race during testing.**

## Skip Races When

- Limited form (too many first starters/unknowns)
- Multiple similar contenders (no clear edge)
- Clear standout but odds don't justify the bet

## Condition Proximity Scale

Track conditions in order (adjacent conditions are highly relevant):

```
G3 → G4 → S5 → S6 → S7 → H8 → H9 → H10
Good ────────────────> Soft ──────────> Heavy
```

**Relevance gradient:**
- Exact condition match = most relevant
- 1 step away = still relevant
- 2 steps away = slightly less relevant
- 3+ steps = diminishing relevance

| Today's Condition | Most Relevant Form | Also Relevant (1 step) |
|-------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| H8 | H8 | S7, H9 |
| S7 | S7 | S6, H8 |
| S5 | S5 | G4, S6 |
| G4 | G4 | G3, S5 |

---

## Understanding the Ratings

| Column | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Rating** | Normalized by distance + condition. 100 = expected speed. |
| **Adj** | Further normalized by track quality. Makes ratings comparable across venues. |

- **Ignore finishing position** - a higher rating is better regardless of where they finished
- **Ignore margin** - already baked into the rating
- **Eased runs (⚠️eased)** - horse wasn't fully pushed, actual ability likely higher than rating shown

## Value Standout Scanner

A separate methodology for finding **value picks** - horses whose recent ratings suggest they're as good as or better than the field, but the odds don't reflect it.

### The Core Idea

Sometimes a horse at $8, $12, $20 has been running Adj ratings recently that are equal to or higher than horses at $2-$4 in the same race. The market hasn't noticed - maybe because of a poor last start (with excuses), or because they're not a "name" horse. These are value standouts.

### How To Scan A Race For Value

**Step 1: What has each horse been running RECENTLY at similar distance/conditions?**

For each runner, look at their last 2-3 race runs (ignore trials) - but prioritise runs at a **similar distance and condition** to today's race. Use the condition proximity scale above (G3-G4-S5-S6-S7-H8-H9-H10). A run at a similar distance and adjacent condition is much more relevant than a run at a completely different distance or opposite end of the track condition scale.

- If today is 1400m S6, a run at 1300m S5 is highly relevant. A run at 1200m G4 is less so. A run at 2000m H8 tells you almost nothing about what they'll do today.
- If a horse's best recent run was at a very different distance/condition, it carries less weight. Their run at a closer distance/condition - even if the Adj is a bit lower - tells you more about what they'll do today.
- Don't ignore a slightly lower-rated run if it was at the right distance and conditions. That's more predictive.

Then apply recency:
- A horse running 101.2, 100.8, 101.0 recently at similar distance = consistently ~101
- A horse who ran 104 six months ago but 98, 99, 98 since = they're a 98-99 horse NOW. The old 104 is irrelevant.
- A horse who ran 97.5 last start but Notes say "Slowly away. Held up. Eased" = that 97.5 understates them. Look at the run before. If it was 101.0, they're probably still a ~101 horse.

**Step 2: Rank the field by recent form at similar distance/conditions**

Sort everyone by what they've actually been running lately **at distances and conditions relevant to today's race**. This is the true form ranking - regardless of what the market says. A horse whose 101 came at 1200m G3 shouldn't rank above a horse whose 100.5 came at 1400m S6 if today's race is 1350m S5.

**Step 3: Find the value mismatch**

Compare the form ranking to the odds:
- Horse running 101+ recently but paying $8+? Interesting.
- $2.50 favourite only running 100.0-100.5 recently, while a $10 shot is running 101+? Value standout.
- Two horses both running ~101 but one is $4 and another is $12? The $12 shot is value.

**Step 4: Check bonus signals**

Once you've found the mismatch:

- **Weight change** - Are they lighter than their recent good runs? Bonus. Significantly lighter (2kg+) = strong bonus.
- **Jockey A/E** - Above 1.0 = bonus. Above 1.2 = strong bonus. Below 0.85 = slight concern.
- **Excuses in recent runs** - If the most recent run was below their usual level AND had stewards notes (eased, held up, wide, checked, slowly away, hampered etc), that run understates their ability. Look at the run BEFORE the excused one - that's closer to their true level. This is how value hides - the market sees "98.5 last start" and drifts them out.
- **First-up record** - If first-up, check their career first-up stats. A horse with a good first-up record coming back fresh is a bonus.

### What Makes A Value Pick

ALL of these:
1. Recent Adj ratings clearly competitive with or above the field (last 2-3 runs, adjusted for excuses)
2. Value odds ($5+ minimum, ideally $8+)
3. No disqualifying red flags

### What Disqualifies

- Only 1 race run (unknown quantity)
- Best Adj was months ago and they've been declining since
- Recent runs at completely different distances/conditions to today
- Race has 50%+ first starters (too many unknowns)

### Common Traps

1. **Cherry-picking old ratings** - They ran 103 once, 8 months ago. Since then: 99, 98, 99. They're a 99 horse now.
2. **Ignoring field context** - 101 means nothing alone. If the field is all 102-103 recently, 101 is below average. If the field is 99-100, 101 is a standout.
3. **Trusting short prices** - $2 doesn't mean best recent form. Check the actual ratings.
4. **Dismissing excused runs** - "98 last start" but stewards said "slowly away, held up, eased". That 98 is meaningless.
5. **Ignoring distance/condition relevance** - A 103 at 1200m G4 is almost irrelevant to a 2000m H8 race. A 100.5 at 1800m S7 tells you far more. Always ask: "did they run this rating at a distance and condition close to today's race?" If not, discount it heavily. Use the condition proximity scale (G3-G4-S5-S6-S7-H8-H9-H10) and distance relevance gradient (within 10% = most relevant, within 20% = still relevant, beyond 30% = limited).

### Output Format

When you find a value standout:
```
PICK: [Horse Name] @ $X.XX
Race: [Track] R[N] - [Distance]m [Condition]

Recent form (Adj): [last 2-3 runs with dates and any notes]
Field comparison: [how their recent form stacks up vs rest of field]
Why value: [why the odds don't match the ratings]
Bonus signals: [weight change, jockey AE, excuses etc]
Risk: [any concerns]
```

When a race has no value standout:
```
SKIP: [Track] R[N] - [reason]
```

### Proven Examples (29-Apr-2026 backtest: 3 winners, 1 second from 5 picks)

On 29 April 2026, this methodology was backtested across Kembla Grange (7 races) and Ipswich (7 races). Results:

| Pick | Odds | Result | Key Reasoning |
|------|------|--------|---------------|
| He'sgotthesay (Ipswich R5) | $12.00 | **WON** | 101.1 Adj at 1630m S5 recent (slowly away excuse). Favs running 96.6-100.2. Clear form edge |

| Blazing Harry (Ipswich R7) | $5.50 | **WON** | 101.5 at 1454m H8, 102.0 at 1500m G4. Better than both favs ($3.70 at 101.3, $3.90 at 100.9) |

#### What the winners had in common:
1. **Recent Adj at relevant distance/conditions was as good as or better than the favourites** - not just "good form somewhere", but form at a distance and condition close to today's race
2. **Excuses explained poor-looking recent runs** - Magical Moments had 3 straight runs with stewards notes. The market saw the finishing positions and drifted the price. The ratings told a different story.
3. **The favourites' form didn't justify their short prices** - He'sgotthesay's favs were running 96-100 recently. Magical Moments' fav had 99.8. The value horse had clearly better form.

#### Example breakdown: He'sgotthesay @ $12.00 (WON - Ipswich R5, 1710m S7)

**His recent form:**
- 101.1 Adj at 1630m S5 (Apr 1) - slowly away, so true form likely higher
- 102.4 Adj at 1600m S7 (Nov 4) - exact condition match, proves he handles wet

**The favourites' recent form:**
- Boom Shot ($3.00): 100.2 Adj at 1615m G4 - G4 is 2 steps from today's S7
- Captain Maverick ($3.90): 96.6 and 99.7 in last 2 starts - terrible

**The mismatch:** He'sgotthesay's recent 101.1 at a relevant distance clearly beats both favs. He also has proven S7 form (102.4). At $12 vs $3-$4, the odds were completely wrong.

#### Example breakdown: Magical Moments @ $11.00 (WON - Kembla R7, 1400m G4)

**Her recent form (all at exactly 1400m):**
- 100.9 Adj at 1400m S7 (Mar 30) - slowly away, held up near 100m
- 100.6 Adj at 1400m G4 (Mar 12) - slowly away
- 101.5 Adj at 1400m G4 (Feb 17) - eased near 1200m (true form higher!)

**Every single run had excuses.** The market saw "slowly away" three times and priced her as a $11 roughie. But the Adj ratings were 100.6-101.5 at the exact distance, with the best run being eased (meaning she was going well and the jockey pulled her up early).

**The favourite:**
- Glastonbury Girl ($3.70): 99.8 Adj at 1400m G4 - clearly below Magical Moments

**The mismatch:** 101.5 (eased, true form higher) vs 99.8. Down 2kg. At $11 vs $3.70.

### Important: Do NOT Use Agent Mode

Backtesting across 8 days showed that delegating race analysis to agent subprocesses produces significantly worse picks than reading the data yourself:

| Method | Days Tested | Picks | Winners |
|--------|-------------|-------|---------|
| **Self-read** | 3 days | 6 | **4 won** ($12, $11, $6.50, $5.50) |
| Agent mode | 4 days | 12+ | **0 won** |

**Why agents fail:** They chase flashy one-off ratings (103+ at country tracks), pick horses stepping to untested distances, and miss the actual winning pattern - consistent form with excuses against weak favourites.

**Why self-read works:** You can properly weigh excuses in stewards notes, judge distance/condition relevance, and spot when a favourite's recent form is actually mediocre.

**Step 1: Export race data**

Do NOT try to read all 8 races at once. Read each race individually, assess it, move on. Focus on BM/Class races (maidens have too many unknowns).

**Step 3: For each race, ask yourself:**
1. What has each horse been running RECENTLY at similar distance/conditions?
2. Is there a horse at $5+ whose consistent recent form matches or beats the $2-$4 favourites?
3. Does the favourite's recent relevant form actually justify their short price?
4. Bonus: weight drop, good jockey A/E, excuses in recent runs?

**Step 4: Output your top 1-3 picks**

Most races should be SKIPs. Only pick when you see the proven pattern:
- Multiple consistent runs at competitive Adj levels (not one-off spikes)
- At relevant distance AND conditions to today's race
- Against favourites whose recent relevant form is mediocre
- Ideally with weight relief, good jockey, and/or excuses hiding true form

## Value Play of the Day (Daily Scan)

A daily product for subscribers: 1-2 value picks per day, scanned across all available meetings.

### When the user says "value scan today" or "find today's value play"

Follow this process:

**Step 1: Get today's meetings**
```bash
source .env && python3 -c "
from api.puntingform import PuntingFormAPI
import json
api = PuntingFormAPI()
meetings = api.get_meetings('DD-MMM-YYYY')
for m in meetings:
    t = m.get('track', {})
    print(f\"{t.get('name', '?')} - {t.get('location', '?')} - {t.get('state', '?')}\")
"
```

**Step 2: Pick the best 2-4 meetings to scan**
- Prioritise metro (M) and provincial (P) tracks — ratings are more reliable
- Skip NZ and HK (no Ladbrokes coverage)
- Skip meetings with mostly maidens
- On quiet days (Sunday etc), scan whatever's available

**Step 3: Export and scan each meeting**
```bash
source .env && python3 tools/export_for_claude_code.py "[Track]" all DD-MMM-YYYY --no-instructions
```

**Step 4: Read each BM/Hcp race one at a time**
- Skip maidens (too many unknowns)
- Follow the Value Standout Scanner methodology above
- Read races yourself — do NOT delegate to agents

**Step 5: Output top 1-2 picks across all meetings**
- Use the standard output format (PICK / SKIP)
- Most races will be SKIPs — that's correct
- If nothing stands out across all meetings, say so (no forced picks)

**Step 6: Log picks to `data/value_plays.json`**
- Append each pick with date, track, race, horse, odds, reasoning, risk
- Set result to null (fill in later when results come in)

### Key Rules
- **$5+ minimum odds** (ideally $8+)
- **Must have multiple consistent runs** at competitive Adj at similar distance/conditions
- **Compare against the favourites' actual recent form** — not just "this horse is good"
- **Read each race yourself** — agents produce bad picks (0/9 vs 4/6 self-read)
- **No forced picks** — some days have nothing. That's fine.
- **1 free pick per day** for subscribers — this is separate from Wednesday/Saturday tipsheets
